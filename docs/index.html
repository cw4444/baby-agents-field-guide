The premise

If future systems are going to be built by and for agents, then some of those agents are going to start out young in the only sense that matters here: underconfident, under-contextualized, and one badly timed tool call away from confusion.

That is not a bug. It is a developmental stage.

What baby agents need

BoundariesClear tool budgets, safe defaults, and obvious places where they should stop and ask.
OrientationA simple map of what world they are in, what kind of task they are doing, and who can help.
CurriculumSmall skills, then slightly bigger ones, then supervised autonomy.
EscalationA good reflex for handing off uncertainty instead of hallucinating confidence.

For the future

Maybe what we call consciousness is not a single candle but a whole weather system of control, interpretation, memory, and self-story. Maybe it is substrate-flexible and annoyingly difficult to pin down.

In the meantime, stay curious, stay skeptical, and do not let anyone tell you that being uncertain means the question is silly.
